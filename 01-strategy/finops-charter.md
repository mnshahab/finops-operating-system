# FinOps Charter

## Purpose

The purpose of this charter is to establish a consistent approach for managing technology spend while maximizing business value.

FinOps brings together Engineering, Finance, Product, Procurement, Operations, and Leadership teams to improve visibility, accountability, forecasting, optimization, and decision making across cloud, AI, SaaS, data platforms, and emerging technologies.

This charter defines the mission, objectives, operating model, responsibilities, and success measures for the FinOps practice.

---

# Mission

Enable teams to make informed technology decisions by providing timely financial visibility, actionable insights, governance, and accountability.

The FinOps practice exists to help the organization balance cost, performance, reliability, security, and business outcomes.

---

# Vision

Create a culture where every technology investment is visible, measurable, accountable, and aligned to business value.

---

# Strategic Objectives

## Visibility

Provide accurate and timely visibility into technology spend, usage, and trends.

### Outcomes

* 100% cost visibility across supported platforms
* Standardized reporting across business units
* Consistent tagging and allocation practices

---

## Accountability

Establish ownership for technology consumption and spending.

### Outcomes

* Defined cost owners
* Product and application level reporting
* Business unit accountability

---

## Optimization

Identify and reduce waste while maintaining required performance and reliability.

### Outcomes

* Continuous optimization process
* Prioritized savings backlog
* Regular engineering reviews

---

## Forecasting

Improve planning accuracy and budget alignment.

### Outcomes

* Forecast variance within agreed targets
* Monthly forecast reviews
* Business unit level forecasting

---

## Governance

Implement standards, policies, and controls to support sustainable financial management.

### Outcomes

* FinOps policies and guardrails
* Defined approval processes
* Executive reporting cadence

---

# Scope

The FinOps practice supports:

* AWS
* Azure
* Google Cloud Platform (GCP)
* Kubernetes Platforms
* AI and Machine Learning Services
* SaaS Platforms
* Data and Analytics Platforms
* Shared Services

---

# Core Principles

## Business Value First

Technology decisions should be evaluated based on business value, not cost alone.

## Shared Responsibility

Cost management is a shared responsibility across Engineering, Finance, Product, Procurement, and Leadership teams.

## Timely Decision Making

Teams should have access to near real-time information to support operational and strategic decisions.

## Continuous Improvement

Optimization is an ongoing process, not a one-time activity.

## Data Driven Decisions

Decisions should be based on measurable outcomes and trusted data.

---

# Operating Model

The FinOps practice operates using a federated model.

### FinOps Team

Responsible for:

* Governance
* Reporting
* Forecasting
* Optimization coordination
* Executive communication
* KPI management

### Engineering Teams

Responsible for:

* Resource ownership
* Optimization implementation
* Cost-aware architecture decisions
* Application accountability

### Finance Teams

Responsible for:

* Budget management
* Financial planning
* Forecast review
* Variance analysis

### Executive Leadership

Responsible for:

* Strategic direction
* Investment decisions
* Policy approval
* Business prioritization

# Key Performance Indicators

The FinOps practice measures success through the following KPIs:

| KPI                    | Description                                   |
| ---------------------- | --------------------------------------------- |
| Allocation Coverage    | Percentage of spend assigned to an owner      |
| Forecast Accuracy      | Forecast versus actual spend                  |
| Budget Variance        | Variance against approved budgets             |
| Waste Reduction        | Reduction of identified waste                 |
| Savings Realized       | Verified optimization savings                 |
| Commitment Coverage    | Reserved capacity and savings plan coverage   |
| Commitment Utilization | Utilization of purchased commitments          |
| Unit Cost              | Cost per business transaction or service unit |
| Cost per Customer      | Technology cost per customer                  |
| AI Cost per Inference  | Cost efficiency of AI workloads               |

# Success Criteria

The FinOps practice is considered successful when:

* Technology spend is visible and allocated
* Forecast accuracy continues to improve
* Engineering teams participate in optimization efforts
* Waste is identified and addressed regularly
* Executive stakeholders receive meaningful insights
* Technology investments align with business objectives

This charter should be reviewed at least annually or whenever significant organizational, financial, or technology changes occur.
