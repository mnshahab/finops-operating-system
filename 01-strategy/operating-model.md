# FinOps Operating Model

## Purpose

The FinOps Operating Model defines how organizations govern, manage, and optimize technology spending across cloud, AI, SaaS, data platforms, and emerging technologies.

The model establishes ownership, decision-making processes, reporting structures, and collaboration patterns between Engineering, Finance, Product, Procurement, Operations, and Executive Leadership.

The objective is to maximize business value while maintaining financial accountability and operational excellence.

---

# Operating Model Principles

## Shared Responsibility

Technology cost management is a shared responsibility across all stakeholders.

Financial accountability should not be owned solely by Finance or the FinOps team.

---

## Business Value Driven

Decisions should balance:

* Cost
* Performance
* Reliability
* Security
* Business outcomes

---

## Transparency

Teams should have visibility into their consumption, costs, forecasts, and optimization opportunities.

---

## Continuous Improvement

Optimization and governance activities should be embedded into normal business operations.

---

# Recommended Model

## Federated FinOps

This repository recommends a Federated FinOps Model.

A central FinOps team provides governance, reporting, standards, and guidance while technology teams remain accountable for consumption and optimization decisions.
